What is planning poker?

Planning poker — also known as scrum poker — is a consensus-based estimation technique widely used in agile software development. Instead of one person guessing how much effort a task takes, the whole team estimates together. Each member privately selects a card representing their estimate (often a Fibonacci number of story points), and everyone reveals at the same time.

The reveal is the interesting part: when estimates differ, the highest and lowest voters explain their reasoning. That short conversation surfaces hidden complexity, misunderstood requirements and edge cases — then the team re-votes until it lands on a shared number. The result is a more accurate estimate and a shared understanding of the work, not just a figure on a ticket.

This tool brings that ritual online for remote and hybrid teams: fast, distraction-free, and free to use.

Frequently asked questions

What is planning poker?

Planning poker (or scrum poker) is a consensus-based estimation technique for agile teams. Each member privately picks a card representing their effort estimate, then everyone reveals at once to discuss differences and agree on a number.

Is this planning poker tool free?

Yes — completely free, with no sign-up, no ads and no tracking. It's also open-source.

Do I need to create an account?

No account is required. Create a room, share the link, and your teammates join instantly with just a name.

Which estimation scales are supported?

Fibonacci, Fibonacci Lite (1, 3, 5, 8, 13), T-shirt sizes, powers of two, and more. You can also enter a fully custom scale.

Can people watch without voting?

Yes. Anyone can join as a spectator to observe a session without casting a vote — useful for facilitators and stakeholders.

Does it work for remote and hybrid teams?

Absolutely. It's built for distributed teams: share a link and everyone estimates together in real time from anywhere.
